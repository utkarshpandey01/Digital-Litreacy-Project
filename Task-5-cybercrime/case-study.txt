UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. In this type of fraud, scammers try to trick people into sending money or sharing their UPI details. The fraud usually starts with a phone call, message, or fake link where the scammer pretends to be a bank officer, customer care executive, or a person sending money. They ask the victim to click on a link or enter their UPI PIN, saying that money will be received. However, instead of receiving money, the victim ends up sending money to the scammer.

Students, elderly people, and people who are not very familiar with digital payments are usually the main targets of this type of fraud. The consequences of UPI fraud include financial loss, stress, and sometimes loss of personal information. Many people lose a large amount of money because they are not aware of these scams.

Therefore, it is very important to spread awareness about UPI fraud and teach people not to share their UPI PIN or OTP with anyone.
